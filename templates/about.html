About the Project

This application, part of a broader research initiative, predicts crime in Chicago based on user location. Developed as a component of a project for the altREU program, it aims to explore and compare the effectiveness of different predictive models in crime forecasting.

  • Celina Anwar - Majoring in Computer Science
  • Sonika Tamilarasan - Majoring in Computer Science and Economics

  • Sumaiya Farook - Majoring in Computer Science and Crop Science

Research Focus

Our research primarily focuses on the application of two advanced predictive models—Random Forest and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) models—to assess and compare their probability accuracies in predicting crime locations. This project not only helps in understanding the dynamic nature of crime but also in evaluating the potential of different machine learning techniques in real-world applications.
